# User Guide - v1.1

**Version**: 1.1.0
**Last Updated**: 2025-01-14

Welcome to v1.1 of the AI Video Generation Platform! This guide will help you understand and use the new features to create better videos.

---

## What's New in v1.1?

v1.1 introduces four powerful new controls that give you more precision over your generated videos:

1. **Video Duration** - Choose how long your video should be
2. **Aspect Ratio** - Select the format that matches your platform
3. **Motion Intensity** - Control how dynamic the movement is
4. **Quality Preset** - Balance speed and quality

All your existing workspaces will continue to work perfectly with sensible defaults applied automatically.

---

## New Features Overview

### 1. Video Duration Control

**What it does**: Choose the length of your generated video.

**Available options**:
- **5 seconds**: Quick action shots, short clips
- **10 seconds**: Standard scenes, most common use case
- **15 seconds**: Extended sequences, longer narratives

**Default**: 5 seconds

**When to use**:
- Use **5 seconds** for quick social media clips or action shots
- Use **10 seconds** for most standard video content
- Use **15 seconds** when you need more time to tell your story

**Tip**: Longer videos take more time to generate. Start with 5 seconds to test your settings, then increase to 10 or 15 for your final video.

---

### 2. Aspect Ratio Selection

**What it does**: Choose the shape and format of your video to match different platforms.

**Available options**:
- **16:9 (Landscape)**: Wide format, horizontal video
- **9:16 (Portrait)**: Tall format, vertical video
- **1:1 (Square)**: Equal width and height
- **4:3 (Classic)**: Traditional TV format

**Default**: 16:9 (Landscape)

**Platform recommendations**:
- **YouTube, Vimeo**: Use 16:9 (Landscape)
- **TikTok, Instagram Reels, Stories**: Use 9:16 (Portrait)
- **Instagram Feed Posts**: Use 1:1 (Square)
- **Traditional TV, Presentations**: Use 4:3 (Classic)

**Tip**: Choose your aspect ratio based on where you plan to share your video. Using the right format ensures your video looks great without black bars.

---

### 3. Motion Intensity Control

**What it does**: Adjusts how dynamic and energetic the movement in your video is.

**Available options**: 1 (Calm) to 5 (Dynamic)
- **1-2 (Calm)**: Subtle, slow movements - peaceful scenes
- **3 (Medium)**: Natural, balanced motion - most versatile
- **4-5 (Dynamic)**: Fast, energetic action - exciting scenes

**Default**: 3 (Medium)

**When to use**:
- **Level 1-2**: Landscapes, peaceful scenes, slow pans, gentle movements
- **Level 3**: General purpose, works well for most content
- **Level 4-5**: Action scenes, sports, dancing, fast-paced content

**Tip**: Match the motion intensity to your content. A sunset scene works better with low intensity (1-2), while a running scene needs high intensity (4-5).

---

### 4. Quality Preset

**What it does**: Lets you choose between faster generation (lower quality) or better quality (slower generation).

**Available options**:
- **Draft** (720p, ~1 minute): Fast previews for testing
- **Standard** (1080p, ~2 minutes): Recommended for most uses
- **High** (1080p+, ~4 minutes): Best quality for final outputs

**Default**: Standard

**When to use**:
- **Draft**: When testing different settings or motion prompts
- **Standard**: For most final videos, good balance of quality and speed
- **High**: For important videos where quality is critical

**Tip**: Use Draft mode to experiment with different parameters quickly. Once you're happy with the settings, switch to Standard or High for your final video.

---

## How to Use the New Features

### Step-by-Step Guide

1. **Create or open a workspace**
   - Click the "+" button to create a new workspace
   - Or click on an existing workspace to edit it

2. **Upload your image**
   - Drag and drop an image, or click to browse
   - Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, GIF

3. **Set your video parameters**:
   - **Duration**: Select 5s, 10s, or 15s from the dropdown
   - **Aspect Ratio**: Click the icon button for your desired format
   - **Motion Intensity**: Drag the slider from 1 (Calm) to 5 (Dynamic)
   - **Quality Preset**: Choose Draft, Standard, or High from the dropdown

4. **Fill in motion description**
   - Describe what movement you want to see
   - Be specific: "Person walking slowly through a park" is better than "walking"

5. **Click "Generate Video"**
   - Your video will start generating
   - Generation time depends on your quality preset
   - You'll see a progress indicator

6. **Wait for completion**
   - Draft: ~1 minute
   - Standard: ~2 minutes
   - High: ~4 minutes

7. **View your video**
   - Once complete, the video player will appear
   - You can download or share your video

## Tips and Best Practices

### Getting the Best Results

1. **Start with Draft Quality**
   - Test your settings with Draft quality first
   - This saves time while you experiment
   - Switch to Standard or High once you're satisfied

2. **Match Duration to Content**
   - Simple actions: 5 seconds
   - Standard scenes: 10 seconds
   - Complex narratives: 15 seconds

3. **Choose the Right Aspect Ratio**
   - Think about where you'll share the video
   - Use 9:16 for mobile-first platforms
   - Use 16:9 for desktop and YouTube

4. **Adjust Motion Intensity**
   - Low intensity (1-2) for calm, peaceful scenes
   - Medium intensity (3) as your default starting point
   - High intensity (4-5) for action and energy

5. **Write Clear Motion Descriptions**
   - Be specific about what you want to see
   - Include details about speed, direction, and style
   - Example: "Person jogging slowly along a beach at sunset" vs "running"

### Common Combinations

**For Social Media (TikTok, Reels)**:
- Duration: 10s
- Aspect Ratio: 9:16 (Portrait)
- Motion Intensity: 4-5 (Dynamic)
- Quality: Standard

**For YouTube Videos**:
- Duration: 10-15s
- Aspect Ratio: 16:9 (Landscape)
- Motion Intensity: 3 (Medium)
- Quality: High

**For Instagram Feed**:
- Duration: 5-10s
- Aspect Ratio: 1:1 (Square)
- Motion Intensity: 3 (Medium)
- Quality: Standard

**For Testing/Previews**:
- Duration: 5s
- Aspect Ratio: Any
- Motion Intensity: 3 (Medium)
- Quality: Draft

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will my old workspaces still work?**
A: Yes! All v1.0 workspaces work perfectly. They'll automatically use default values for the new parameters (5s duration, 16:9 aspect ratio, medium motion intensity, standard quality).

**Q: Can I change the parameters after generating a video?**
A: Yes! You can adjust any parameter and generate a new video. Your changes are saved automatically.

**Q: Why are the duration options 5/10/15 seconds?**
A: These are the durations supported by our AI video generation engine (Qwen API). They provide good coverage for most use cases.

**Q: How long does video generation take?**
A: It depends on your quality preset:
- Draft: ~1 minute
- Standard: ~2 minutes
- High: ~4 minutes

**Q: What happens if I close the browser while generating?**
A: Your video will continue generating on the server. When you return, you'll see the completed video.

**Q: Can I generate multiple videos at once?**
A: Yes! Each workspace can generate independently. You can start multiple generations across different workspaces.
